What are SMART goals?

Simply put, they are statements of the important results you are working to accomplish.

What is the SMART criteria?

Specific

Contemplate what needs to be accomplished and the actions that are necessary to do so.

Measurable

Think about how a goal and its preceding milestones will be measured.

Achievable

Honestly consider whether or not a particular goal is actionable. In particular, the skills needed to realise the goal and if they are skills you possess.

Relevant

Deliberate the importance of achieving what you have in mind and how it aligns with your broader goals.

Time-bound

Consider the timeframe for accomplishing your goal.

How do I decide the right scope for my goals?

SMART goals are designed to address all of your major academic or professional responsibilities. Bear in mind, goals are intended to focus attention and resources on all that is important so that you can be successful in achieving your priorities. Specifically, SMART goals are those for the workplace.

Where do I start?

  1. Start by evaluating your specific role within your organisation and the broad areas of responsibility and results for which you are accountable.

  2. Develop over-arching goal statements for each bucket. Keep in mind, in order to get scope right, you need to prioritise end results as opposed to individual tasks.

  3. Goals should be high level enough to encompass the core outcomes for which you are responsible. However, they must simultaneously be specific and clear enough so that you are able to measure success.

  4. Evaluate all ongoing responsibilities, new projects, assignments, priorities or initiatives. Your goals should be focused on the aforementioned.

  5. Having too many goals can be an indicator that your goals are scoped at too low of a level and are focused more on tasks as opposed to end results.

  6. If it seems that your goals are becoming too numerous and task-oriented, it may be helpful to consider combining several goal statements into a broader outcome area.
