When goal-setting, be specific about what you want to accomplish. Consider this the mission statement for your goal. Bear in mind, this isn't a detailed list of how you're going to meet a goal. Rather, it should include an answer to one of the following types of questions:

  • Who – Consider all those who need to be involved in order to achieve the goal. This is particularly important when you're working on a group project.

  • What – Think about exactly what you are trying to accomplish, being as descriptive as possible.

  • When – This question is answered more specifically in the time-bound part of creating a SMART goal. That said, you still need to identify a roundabout timeframe.

  • Where – This may not always apply, especially when you're setting personal goals. However, if there is a specific location or relevant event, be sure to identify it here.

  • Which – Determine any and all related obstacles or requirements that you will need to overcome in order to achieve your goal. This can be particularly beneficial when it comes to deciding if your goal is realistic.

  • Why – Consider why achieving the goal is important to you as an individual.
